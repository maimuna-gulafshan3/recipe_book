Dal Rice, a comforting Indian staple, combines fluffy white rice with creamy toor dal (split pigeon peas), On the side, Fried Mutton, prepared with tender cuts of mutton, sautéed onions, and a medley of aromatic spices, tantalizes the taste buds with its rich, savory flavors. Slow-cooked to perfection, this dish is a delightful complement to the simplicity of dal rice. Completing the meal is the versatile Chapathi (Roti),Together, this trio forms a harmonious and hearty meal cherished in households across India.

Dal rice with fried mutton

this trio forms a harmonious and hearty meal cherished in households across India.

                      

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup toor dal
  • 3½ cup water
  • 1 tsp mustard / rai
  • 1 tsp jeera / cumin
  • 1 dried red chilli, broken
  • few curry leaves
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp coriander, finely chopped
     

Simple steps and you are ready with your healthy and delicious Breakfast

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat 3 tablespoons of cooking oil in a pan or pressure cooker. Add finely chopped onions and sauté them until they turn translucent.
  2. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste to the onions and continue to sauté for another couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears and the onions turn golden brown.
  3. add in 1 onion followed by 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and 1 green chilli. saute well.
  4. further add ¼ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp chilli powder and ½ tsp coriander powder. saute on low flame.
  5. Incorporate Tomatoes and Spices: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Fry the Mutton: Add the 500 grams of mutton pieces to the pan and fry them until they are browned on all sides.
  7. Cook Until Tender: If using a pressure cooker, add a little water, close the lid, and cook for about 3-4 whistles or until the mutton is tender.
